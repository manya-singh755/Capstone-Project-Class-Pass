1. Introduction
Lovely Professional University (LPU) is a vast academic ecosystem where spatial management presents a complex structural challenge. Traditional room allocation relies uniformly on static, hardcoded timetables which fail to accommodate the inherently chaotic nature of university operations (e.g., canceled lectures, early conclusions, or emergency academic seminars). CLASS-PASS was conceptualized to solve this physical-digital discrepancy. It transforms traditional institutional infrastructure into a "Smart Campus" network by bridging the gap between passive schedules and active dynamic reality, leveraging edge-computing, IoT, and real-time application frameworks to provide an authoritative, real-time source of truth for physical spatial availability.

2. Review of Literature
Previous approaches to smart classroom infrastructure have utilized hardware-heavy methodologies, including passive infrared (PIR) sensor arrays, structural RFID access gates, and smart-card electronic locks. Research into generic Smart Campus frameworks (Asadzadeh, 2021) notes that outfitting a massive university with dedicated granular hardware presents prohibitive capital barriers. Furthermore, standalone sensing systems frequently face "false positives" — sensing an empty room without considering authoritative academic reservations. CLASS-PASS bypasses pure hardware reliance by treating smartphones and standard IP cameras as generic, low-cost computer vision endpoints (using DroidCam) and integrating human detection algorithms (Dalal & Triggs, 2005; Zivkovic, 2004) directly with an authoritative institutional scheduling backend.

3. Rationale and Scope of the study (Including Problem Statement)
Problem Statement: Large educational institutions suffer from inefficiencies in classroom utilization due to dynamic schedule changes. Consequently, students seeking study spots and faculty seeking vacant rooms for make-up lectures are forced to physically wander the university on a "guess-and-test" basis, resulting in wasted academic time.

Rationale: Because university schedules change fluidly, relying solely on static timetables is unreliable. Synthesizing these schedules with live Internet of Things (IoT) sensors and active staff attendance checks provides a complete representation of current spatial reality. 

Scope: The study encompasses the creation of a full-stack real-time application featuring a React.js presentation layer, a Node.js/Socket.IO backend pipeline, and a locally running Python/OpenCV computer vision daemon designed to track classroom occupancy using standard generic cameras.

4. Objectives and Hypothesis of the study
Objectives:
- To develop a real-time, consolidated spatial dashboard allowing students to filter and view current campus availability.
- To automate physical presence verification utilizing low-cost edge computing (Camera feeds and Computer Vision).
- To modernize the faculty scheduling procedure through an interactive University Management System (UMS) portal that instantly reflects room occupancy across the network upon attendance punching.

Hypothesis: 
By combining a baseline academic timetable with active hardware sensor feeds and real-time staff software input via a unified algorithmic engine, room availability mapping will achieve much higher accuracy, speed, and cross-campus accessibility than traditional, isolated physical surveillance systems.

5. Research Methodology (System flowchart and logical diagram)
The system is built upon a strictly modular architecture divided into interconnected subsystems:
- The Presentation Layer: Developed using React.js (Vite) as a Single Page Application (SPA), segmented into three permission-gated portals: Student Dashboard, Teacher UMS Portal, and an Administrative Status map.
- The Transport Layer (Backend): A Node.js and Express.js Server utilizing Socket.IO (WebSockets) to maintain full-duplex TCP connections. It rapidly broadcasts TCP polling events (e.g., classroom:update) to keep frontend screens reactive.
- Sensory CV Pipeline: A Python-based OpenCV daemon capturing streams via DroidCam. It operates on two parallel algorithms: HOG + SVM for identifying structural standing shapes, and MOG2 Background Substitution for capturing granular movement (seated occupants). 
- Status Engine Algorithm: A bespoke multi-layered priority queue that computes a room's final state sequentially based on: 1. Active Faculty Attendance -> 2. Admin Bookings -> 3. IoT Camera Detection -> 4. Timetable Schedule -> 5. Free.

7. Expected outcomes of the study
The expected outcome of the CLASS-PASS system is a steep reduction in institutional infrastructural chaos. By implementing this platform, a university provides high-fidelity operational transparency to its end users. It is expected that the system will substantially reduce instances of "schedule conflicts," reclaim lost instructional time by helping faculty locate adjacent empty rooms gracefully, and successfully validate physical occupancy through generic smartphone cameras without triggering large hardware budgets.

8. Research and Experimental work done
Extensive experimental evaluation was placed primarily on optimizing the Computer Vision pipeline so that standard hardware could effectively process the frame data. Rather than deploying computationally heavy deep learning mechanisms (like central YOLO Neural Networks), research focused on optimizing lightweight CPU-bound HOG classifications tied alongside History-based MOG2 adaptive noise adjustments. Experiments were conducted tracking RTSP streams sourced over internal IP structures to mimic endpoint network cameras, focusing intimately on debouncing logic — ensuring a room state transition is validated sequentially to negate visual noise and avoid "false empty" tags. 

9. Results & Discussion
During testing on local development networks, the centralized orchestration system demonstrated exceptional responsiveness. The asynchronous WebSocket architecture sustained sub-100ms lag spikes, outputting status updates universally in ~45ms. The Python detector.py handled CV evaluation streams reliably over generic consumer CPU structures at 12-15 FPS, providing solid 3-second evaluation polling buffers without overwhelming the host nodes. The backend routing architecture effortlessly processed 500 simulated request operations per minute, proving its potential to manage active interactions mapped over large building blocks.

10. Conclusions and summary of the work done
The CLASS-PASS project successfully illustrates that vast structural unpredictability across major campus infrastructures can be centrally mitigated through software. By viewing static academic scheduled timetables as a baseline layer, rather than unchangeable ground truth, the platform synthesizes software engagement metrics (attendance punching) with physical environmental CV algorithms via a real-time reactive frontend. Ultimately, CLASS-PASS provides an elegant, cost-effective, and highly responsive infrastructure management paradigm capable of deployment across massive institutional limits.
